The situation

You've built something real. The career. The reputation. The life you were told to build. And yet there's a distance — between you and the people you built it for — that the same skills which built everything else cannot seem to close.

It's a Tuesday evening. Back-to-back meetings since eight. Your phone is in your pocket and you're aware of it the way you're aware of a low-grade headache. You're home. Your body is in the room. But if someone asked you what your partner just said — you couldn't tell them. Not because you don't care. Because the part of you that listens is still at the office.

Your son comes downstairs. Quiet. Something is off. You ask the right questions — with the same skill you'd use to manage a difficult stakeholder. He gives one-word answers. Goes back to his room. You think he wouldn't open up. But that isn't what happened. What happened is you turned a moment where he needed to be received into a moment where he was being managed.

Is this for you

This programme is for the man who has noticed the distance — and is ready to close it.

You are successful. You are providing. You are showing up in every way the role requires. But there is a gap between the man your family experiences and the man you thought you'd be by now.

You may have tried things before — quietly, without telling anyone. And it felt wrong. Not painful. Wrong. Like the tool didn't match what needed fixing. Your resistance was accurate, not avoidance.

This programme is not for everyone.

Not for men in acute crisis. Not group therapy. Not for men who want tips, techniques, or a weekend fix.

What it requires: six Tuesday evenings. Honesty. The willingness to be in a room where the professional mask doesn't work. If that feels right — even slightly — you're the man it was built for.

Questions you're probably asking yourself

Is this therapy? +
No. This is coaching and group work, not therapy. The distinction matters. Therapy typically explores the past to understand the present. This programme builds specific capacities in the present and applies them directly to your life at home. If clinical support is what you need, Richard will tell you — honestly and directly — during the intake conversation.
I'm not sure I'm struggling enough for this. +
Most men who join this programme are not in crisis. They are performing well by every available measure. What they've noticed is a distance — at the dinner table, with their partner, with their children — that doesn't show up in any review. If you've noticed it, that's enough.
Is it confidential? +
Completely. What's shared in the group stays in the group. Every man agrees to this before the first session. The men in this programme are professionals who understand what confidentiality means. Your professional reputation is safe.
What if I can't make every session? +
The programme is designed as a continuous arc — each week builds on the last. Missing a session weakens your experience and the group's. If your schedule can't accommodate six consecutive weeks, wait for a cohort whose dates work.
What happens on Zoom that's different from every other Zoom call? +
Everything. Camera on. No second screens. No multitasking. The work involves your body, your voice, your face — not a passive presentation. Most men are surprised by how quickly the screen disappears and the room becomes real.
How is this different from what I've tried before? +
Most of what's available starts from the assumption that something is wrong with you — that you're shut down, emotionally unavailable, behind. This programme starts from the recognition that the qualities which made you successful were intelligent responses to the environments that shaped you. The distance at home is not a character flaw. It's a competency gap — and competency gaps close. The man who tried to open up and found it didn't fit wasn't failing. He was using a tool that was never built for him.
