You're not in crisis. So why does the man at home feel like a stranger?

The problem isn't your career. It isn't your marriage. It's that the operating mode that made you effective at work doesn't translate at the kitchen table — and nobody handed you a manual for the switch.

You've been carrying a posture, a tempo, a way of being in the room that's served you. It walks home with you. And it costs you.

You become the man people seek out at work and the one your daughter learns not to interrupt.

That's not a flaw. That's a system you built. And systems can be rebuilt.
